Final Project: Fluid Simulator

Eric Li, Shamus Li, Hailey Park, Omar Yu | CS 184 Spring 2023

Short Summary: Our goal is to create working 3d fluid simulation. We aim to create a scene where different objects are dropped into a body of water confined by a transparent cube. The final product is an animation and will involve both computing the physics of the water and rendering of it.

Problem Description

Fluid simulation is an important problem since we would like to model fluids in our animations and renders. It is challenging however because fluids are modeled by complicated physics equations that are computationally expensive to compute. We aim to solve this by implementing a 3-dimensional particle based simulation.

Goals and Deliverables

Goals

  • By implementing this fluid simulation, we hope to answer these questions:
    • Can we accurately simulate fluid mechanics?
    • Can we make it look good?
    • Can we fulfill these goals without having it be too computationally expensive?

Planned Deliverables

  • Make a working fluid simulator (particle-based)
    • Compute collisions of water
    • Render the water
    • Make optimizations to make the code run fast
      • We will measure performance by timing the rendering speed for our simulation, and potentially visualize this with a graph showing speedup
  • Simulate dropping multiple different objects into a body of water
    • Will simulate how objects of different sizes/shapes/mass affect the splash simulation
    • Bounding box of the scene/water should be small to optimize
